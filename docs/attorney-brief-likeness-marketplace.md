# DemandOrchestrator — Attorney Brief (Business Plan + Risk Review Request)

## Purpose of this Document
This brief is intended for legal counsel review. We are requesting guidance on legal structure, contractual language, risk controls, and compliance requirements for a product that generates social media content (including AI presenter videos) for businesses.

---

## 1) Company + Product Summary

**Company:** CrossCurrent Ventures LLC (operating product: DemandOrchestrator)

**Product:** A marketing automation platform that helps users generate, review, schedule, and publish social media content across platforms (starting with X, then LinkedIn/Instagram/video channels).

**Core capabilities in current/near-term roadmap:**
- Draft generation from user prompts + uploaded context (documents/images) + web research signals
- Batch queue generation (e.g., 8-post cadence queue)
- Cadence scheduling presets for approved drafts
- Video script generation and AI presenter rendering
- Presenter options:
  1. User’s own likeness (self-captured intro video)
  2. Synthetic presenters (platform-provided AI presenter catalog)
  3. Licensed human presenters (future model marketplace)

---

## 2) Business Model (Current + Planned)

### A) SaaS subscription tiers
- **Starter/Pro:** core drafting + scheduling features
- **Top Tier:** includes synthetic presenter directory access
- **Corporate:** multi-seat/team avatar management, role permissions, shared workflows

### B) Marketplace / usage monetization (planned)
- Licensed presenter marketplace for real human models
- Customers pay to use specific model likeness
- Platform takes a fee; model receives payout per usage/license terms

---

## 3) Likeness + Presenter Framework

## 3.1 Presenter Types
1. **Synthetic presenters**
   - Fully AI-generated personas, no real human identity rights assignment
2. **Licensed real-human presenters**
   - Real individuals who opt in via consent + waiver + licensing agreement

## 3.2 Consent + Rights Controls (planned requirements)
- Explicit informed consent for likeness usage
- Defined permitted uses (category menu + restrictions)
- Explicit prohibited uses
- Territory/language restrictions
- Duration/term and revocation triggers
- Compensation and payout terms
- Recordkeeping and audit trail for each generated usage

---

## 4) Key Risk Areas Requiring Legal Guidance

## 4.1 Right of publicity / likeness misuse
**Risk:** A model’s likeness used outside agreed purpose, category, geography, or term.
**Need guidance on:**
- License grant scope language
- Category/industry restrictions and enforceability
- Revocation + takedown obligations
- Indemnity allocation

## 4.2 Deepfake / deceptive content risk
**Risk:** AI presenter used to imply false endorsement or impersonation.
**Need guidance on:**
- Required disclosure language (when/how)
- Banned use policy drafting
- Enforcement terms + immediate suspension rights
- Jurisdiction-specific deepfake/impersonation laws

## 4.3 Advertising/consumer protection compliance
**Risk:** Misleading claims, undisclosed synthetic presenter usage, testimonial issues.
**Need guidance on:**
- FTC-style endorsement/disclosure rules and equivalents
- Platform policy alignment (X/Meta/YouTube/TikTok)
- Required user warranties re claims/substantiation

## 4.4 IP and copyright risk
**Risk:** User uploads infringing materials; generated copy/images may create IP disputes.
**Need guidance on:**
- DMCA/takedown process design
- User-generated content licenses
- Repeat infringer policy
- Ownership/license language for outputs

## 4.5 Defamation, misinformation, regulated-content risk
**Risk:** Generated scripts include harmful or non-compliant statements.
**Need guidance on:**
- Terms + policy language for prohibited content
- High-risk category controls (politics, medical, financial)
- Safe-harbor operations process

## 4.6 Data privacy / biometric concerns
**Risk:** Handling video uploads that may qualify as biometric/sensitive data.
**Need guidance on:**
- Data classification + retention policy
- Consent logging standards
- Deletion timelines + user rights handling
- Cross-border processing concerns

## 4.7 Employment/contractor classification (model payouts)
**Risk:** Misclassification of participating models.
**Need guidance on:**
- Contractor agreement structure
- Tax reporting responsibilities
- Marketplace payout/legal entity setup

---

## 5) Operational Guardrails We Intend to Implement

- Hard content policy checks before render/publish
- Model-level allowed/prohibited category matrix
- Mandatory human approval before posting (no unsupervised auto-publish)
- Full generation audit logs (user, model, prompt/script, timestamp, output ID)
- Immediate kill switch per model/license/customer workspace
- Violation triage + suspension workflow

## 6) Specific Questions for Counsel

1. What is the safest contract structure for licensed human presenters (consent + licensing + payout)?
2. What disclosures should be mandatory for AI-generated presenter content?
3. What categories should be prohibited by default to reduce liability?
4. What indemnity split is market-standard between platform, model, and customer?
5. What revocation/takedown SLA and process language should be used?
6. What privacy/biometric obligations apply to uploaded intro videos?

## 8) Launch Posture Recommendation (Proposed)

**Phase 1 (lower risk):**
- Synthetic presenters only
- No real-person model marketplace yet

**Phase 2 (controlled pilot):**
- Invite-only human model marketplace
- Strict category limits
- Manual review layer
